What is sustainability?

According to the UN World Commission on Environment and Development, "sustainable development is development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs".

Sustainable practices support ecological, human, and economic health and vitality. Sustainability presumes that resources are finite, and should be used conservatively and wisely with a view to long-term priorities and consequences of the ways in which resources are used. In simplest terms, sustainability is about our children and our grandchildren, and the world we will leave them.

How do you adopt a sustainable lifestyle?

To live sustainably, we must understand how our lifestyle choices impact the world around us and find ways for everyone to live better and lighter. Living a sustainable lifestyle means making choices that will positively impact the planet tomorrow, such as buying regional products; reducing energy consumption; using a bike or public transportation instead of a private vehicle; efficient use of water when washing, cleaning, cooking, etc; bringing your own bag to avoid plastic bags at stores; and so on.

By implementing these simple actions in your daily life, you can reduce your carbon footprint to be as small as possible and minimize your use of Earth's resources.


What are the long-term effects of not living sustainably?

  • More landfills popping up everywhere.
  • More animals going extinct due to deforestation and pollution.
  • An increase in respiratory diseases.
  • Harsher weather (drier and hotter summers, colder and harsher winters, more tropical storms).
  • Rising sea levels.
  • Worsening living conditions for lower-income communities, as there is more trash, worse air, and so on.
  • Declining soil quality, and likely the reduced nutritional quality of our food.
  • Fewer green spaces to enjoy in cities and across the world.
