Sustainable Polymers

Polymers are essential in various aspects of our daily life due to their low-cost, durability, flexibility, and moldability, but their huge and unbalanced manufacturing processes and non-degradability behavior have put a burden by polluting the environment due to their single-use and throw them anywhere. Synthetic polymers are derived from petroleum oil, and made by scientists and engineers, hence the term "synthetic". These plastics are non-biodegradable as their long polymer chains are too stable and complex to be processed by microorganisms, taking hundreds of years before they can fully break down. Their long-term presence leads to soil, water and air pollution, affecting ecosystems, negatively impacting animals, plants, and even humans' health.
